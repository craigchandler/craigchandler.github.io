About Craig Chandler

Hi, I'm Craig Chandler. I've spent over 25 years working with customers to design and develop analytical tools for capacity planning, scheduling, and risk management using a range of platforms.

I have been a member of the InterDynamics Application Development and Decision Support team since December 1999 and have been involved in a range of activities from supply chain modeling, supply chain strategy assessment, human fatigue algorithm implementation, through to the development of the core Planimate® simulation/development platform.

Over the years, I've gained extensive experience in modeling customizable supply chain networks and I have designed and provided analytical tools across a range of industries including groups such as DSTO, Coles Myer, Alcoa, Hitachi, Orica Explosives, and Port Authority of NY & NJ. Rail modeling experience includes capacity planning models for BHP Billiton iron ore in Western Australia, Aurizon coal, Queensland Rail bulk freight, CBG bauxite rail in West Africa, and Inteplan coal rail operations in the Hunter Valley.

Mineral Resources Ltd Iron Ore Capacity Model (2023)

Developed a visually informative and calibrated simulation model for the Iron Ore operations of Mineral Resources Ltd. The model demonstrates the dynamics and interactions of the iron ore supply chain from the ROM stockpile through to Ship Loading.

  • Determined system capacity under initial and target process times and infrastructure.
  • Supported analysis for determining future capacity under various operating scenarios.
  • Enabled studies of the impacts of various maintenance schedules and system outages.
  • Provided outputs including ship loaded tonnes, cycle times, and utilization reports for all assets and transport fleets.

Bowen Rail Capacity Model (2023)

Developed a visually informative and calibrated simulation model for Bowen Rail's rail operations. The model demonstrates the dynamics and interactions of train cycles within the Aurizon and CRN railway networks as part of the broader logistics supply chain.

  • Provided insights into interactions between trains and system capacity under current and future process times and infrastructure.
  • Supported analysis for determining future capacity under various operating scenarios.
  • Enabled studies of the impacts of maintenance schedules and system outages.
  • Outputs included train cycle times, tonnage throughput rates, utilization reports, and more.
